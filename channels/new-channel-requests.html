Karsus 04-Dec-21 11:34 PM
I feel like we could use more granularity around the open questions from the doc to keep discussions more clear and focused. So here are some more channel suggestions: #vetting-founders: How can you be sure that this person is who they say they are, and that they can execute? Technical skills aside, do they know the problem space? Are they passionate? #vetting-projects: What should this process look like? How far can this be automated? Who will validate and how do you know that you can trust them? #vetting-investors: Do we need to worry about dodgy people getting into the system or receiving benefits later on? If returns were in crypto, all kinds of anonymous players could enter into the game - some of them being people Americans aren't allowed to do business with (not that this isn't fun to ignore ) There's also a question of what else the investors can offer beyond money. #increasing-win-rate: Maybe the founders have weaknesses and aren't going to be able to succeed without certain kinds of support, but the project is still worth doing. Who or what is needed to increase the likelihood of project success? #tracking-startup-progress: How should tracking of progress and releasing more money on hitting milestones be handled? Should there be a common framework for assessment, with supporting tooling? #international-funding: Are there key limitations in certain areas that are worth considering and finding workarounds for? #restricting-scope: Would this model be better suited to certain kinds of businesses, or businesses who need specific kinds of support initially? What kinds of projects might be easier to vet initially (depending on skills of early members of the network)? #accelerating-network-learning: As in, what frameworks if established, would make it easier to learn how to do this better? ... This is where the AI enhancements and other tracking of data come in. (edited)
Karsus 04-Dec-21 11:45 PM
#ethics: There should be some high level guidelines. Although problems usually aren't meaningfully revealed until you start becoming bigger, and being too quick to add restrictions can restrict the good along with the bad. A high level framework for tracking impact might be cool to think about. #summaries: Unlikely to happen, but someone summarizing some of the key ideas discussed so far for different sections, would make it easier for new users to enter those discussions. [Moderator only?] #insights: If people think that a point raised so far is kinda amazing and worth it for everyone to know, it should be added here. [Moderator only?] (edited)
