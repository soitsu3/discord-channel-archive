seanmcdonald.eth 15-Dec-21 08:26 PM
This is great. I have several areas to add additional questions. I could comment on the google doc but thought it would be clearer here: 1) A lot of mentor matching comes down to availability. A question set or time grid about their availability and overall time commitment will save a lot of trouble: *What time zone are you in? *How many hours per month can you commit? *Are you available during the work day, or only nights & weekends? *Is mentoring / advising a part of your paid professional responsibility set? 2) Another big driver of mentor relationship success is affinity matching, or psychographic matching. A question set about values and interests will increase successful matching: *Do you have an industry or interest niche to which you are narrowly focused? *Do you have a preference to work with founders of diverse background? *Are you a female or diverse background founder who is more comfortable with culturally similar mentors? *Do you have professional restrictions that require advisement to be outside of certain categories, for example if you manage a third party's portfolio or IP and cannot work in that domain? 3) Last success driver is value-add, so questions like: *Can you provide access to resources, discounts, or other special assistance to founders? *Do you have a media platform or large audience you are willing to use to support founders? *Are you willing to publicly promote the company? *Are you interested in receiving commissions and referrals for goals you accomplish for the company? fwiw I served on the board of a national mentorship nonprofit that structured thousands of executive relationships each year, and my wife started and incubator that has facilitated about four thousand entrepreneur / advisor relationship over the past ten years.
